"The Killers" is a short story by Ernest Hemingway, first published in 1927. The story is set in a small town in the American Midwest, and it tells the story of two hitmen who come to town to kill a man named George Wilson. The story is told from the point of view of Nick Adams, a young man who is friends with Wilson.

"The Killers" is a classic example of Hemingway's spare and economical writing style. The story is only about 10 pages long, but it packs a powerful punch. The characters are well-developed, and the plot is suspenseful and exciting. The story also explores themes of violence, death, and the inevitability of fate.

"The Killers" is one of Hemingway's most famous stories, and it has been adapted into several films and television shows. The story is a classic of American literature, and it is sure to entertain and enlighten readers for generations to come.

The writing is spare and economical. Hemingway's prose is clear and concise, and he uses only the words that are necessary to tell the story.
